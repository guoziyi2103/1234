Daily Essentials 生活必需

Internet & SIM

Stay connected during your stay

  • Hotel WiFi: Free high-speed
  • Local SIM: China Mobile/Unicom
  • VPN needed for Google/Facebook
  • WeChat for daily communication

Money & Payment

Payment methods in China

  • WeChat Pay & Alipay (most common)
  • Cash: RMB (Yuan)
  • Credit cards (limited acceptance)
  • Hotel accepts international cards

Language

Communication tips

  • Download translation apps
  • Learn basic phrases
  • Hotel staff speaks English
  • Use translation devices

Pro Tip

Download WeChat and Alipay before arriving. Set up your payment methods to make daily transactions easier.

Food & Dining Guide 美食指南

Must-Try Local Dishes

  • Peking Duck - 北京烤鸭
  • Hot Pot - 火锅
  • Dim Sum - 点心
  • Soup Dumplings - 小笼包
  • Kung Pao Chicken - 宫保鸡丁

Cafes & Restaurants

  • Starbucks (major cities)
  • Local tea houses
  • McDonald's/KFC (widely available)
  • Pizza Hut, Domino's
  • Hotel restaurant (international menu)

Grocery Shopping

  • Walmart, Carrefour
  • Local supermarkets
  • Fresh markets for produce
  • Import stores for western products
  • Bottled water recommended

Food Safety

Drink bottled water, avoid street food initially, and choose busy restaurants for better food safety.

Shopping Guide 购物指南

Shopping Malls

  • International brands available
  • Price comparison recommended
  • Sales seasons: Jan/Feb, Jul/Aug
  • Tax refund for tourists

Local Markets

  • Pearl markets - 珍珠市场
  • Silk markets - 丝绸市场
  • Antique markets - 古董市场
  • Bargaining expected

Souvenirs

Transportation 交通出行

Public Transport

  • Metro: Fast, efficient, cheap
  • Bus: Extensive network
  • Transportation cards available
  • Apps: Didi (Uber equivalent)

Taxis & Ride-sharing

  • Official taxis (metered)
  • Didi Chuxing app
  • Keep receipts
  • Show destination in Chinese

Bike Sharing

  • Mobike, Ofo, Hello Bike
  • QR code activation
  • Very cheap (1-2 RMB/hour)
  • Bike lanes in major cities

Culture & Etiquette 文化礼仪

Social Etiquette

  • Two-handed business card exchange
  • Respect for elders
  • Moderate speaking volume
  • Ask permission before photos

Dining Etiquette

  • Wait for host to start eating
  • Toast before drinking
  • Don't tap bowl with chopsticks
  • Tipping not customary

Important Dates

  • Chinese New Year (Jan/Feb)
  • Mid-Autumn Festival
  • Dragon Boat Festival
  • National Day (Oct 1-7)

Cultural Note

Face (面子) is important in Chinese culture. Avoid causing others to lose face in public situations.
